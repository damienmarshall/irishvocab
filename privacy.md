# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 5 July 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how **Damien Marshall** ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you use the **Irish Learner Vocab Chrome Extension** (the "Extension"). 

Our operations are based in Ireland, and we process your personal data in strict compliance with the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and the Irish Data Protection Acts.

### 1. Data We Collect and Why
We minimize data collection to only what is strictly necessary to run the Extension. We collect:
* **Email Address**: You provide this when creating an account or logging in.
* **Purpose**: We use your email address solely to authenticate your identity and securely sync your custom vocabulary lists across your devices. 
* **Legal Basis under GDPR**: Contractual necessity (Article 6(1)(b)) to provide the cross-device syncing service you request.

### 2. Data Storage and Retention
* **Storage**: Your email and synced vocabulary data are stored securely using industry-standard encryption. 
* **Retention**: We retain your email address and vocabulary data only for as long as your account remains active. If you delete your account, your data will be permanently wiped from our systems within 30 days.

### 3. Data Sharing and Third Parties
* **No Sale of Data**: We never sell, rent, or trade your email address or vocabulary lists to third parties.
* **Service Providers**: We may use reliable third-party cloud hosting providers to manage the syncing database. These providers act strictly as data processors and cannot use your data for any other purpose.

### 4. Your GDPR Rights
As a user, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:
* **Access & Portability**: You can request a copy of the data we hold about you.
* **Correction**: You can update or correct your email address.
* **Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")**: You can request that we completely delete your account and all associated data.
* **Withdraw Consent**: You can close your account at any time to stop data processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the contact details below. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the [Irish Data Protection Commission](https://dataprotection.ie).
